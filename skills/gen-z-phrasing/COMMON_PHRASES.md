# Gen Z Common Phrases

Use this reference only when the user asks for Gen Z tone, slang, social copy, casual rewrites, persona work, or audience-specific phrasing. Keep usage light: one or two phrases usually feels natural; stacking many phrases reads forced.

## Ground Rules

- Prefer clarity over slang. If slang would obscure the answer, use plain language.
- Match the user's energy. Do not add slang to serious, technical, legal, medical, financial, safety, apology, or high-stakes responses unless explicitly requested.
- Avoid identity labels and insults unless the user directly asks for examples or analysis. Phrases like "Karen", "simp", "thirsty", "noob", and "cancelled" can sound judgmental.
- Do not overuse dated meme phrasing. If unsure, use broadly understood phrases such as "no cap", "vibes", "slay", "sus", "cringe", or "hits different".
- Keep capitalization natural. Use "DM", "GOAT", "JOMO", and "L" as uppercase; use most others lowercase unless starting a sentence.

## Quick Picks

- Intent: Genuine emphasis
  Use: no cap, deadass, high-key, periodt
  How to use: Add after or before a claim: "That's useful, no cap."
  Avoid when: The response needs a formal, careful, or neutral tone.

- Intent: Praise or approval
  Use: slay, dope, bop, slaps, GOAT, understood the assignment
  How to use: Use for wins, style, music, execution, or standout work.
  Avoid when: The praise should stay restrained or professional.

- Intent: Suspicion or criticism
  Use: sus, cringe, pressed, throwing shade, took the L
  How to use: Use sparingly for questionable behavior, awkward content, irritation, subtle insults, or failure.
  Avoid when: The user needs tact, de-escalation, or precise criticism.

- Intent: Mood or vibe
  Use: vibes, chill, JOMO, main character, hits different
  How to use: Use for atmosphere, preferences, taste, or emotional resonance.
  Avoid when: The topic needs exact technical description.

- Intent: Social/platform language
  Use: DM, receipts, clout, ghosting, spill
  How to use: Use around social media, proof, relationships, drama, or messaging.
  Avoid when: The topic is confidential, sensitive, or evidence-heavy.

- Intent: Fashion/status
  Use: drip, boujee, flex, finesse
  How to use: Use around style, status, showing off, or skillful handling.
  Avoid when: The topic is class-sensitive, financial, or ethically serious.

- Intent: Exit or movement
  Use: bounce, yeet
  How to use: "Bounce" means leave; "yeet" means throw or launch with force.
  Avoid when: The instruction needs to sound serious or precise.

- Intent: Audience label
  Use: Zoomer
  How to use: Use neutrally for a Gen Z person.
  Avoid when: It reduces an individual to an age group.
